Balancing Car

A self-balancing motor wheel control project focused on chassis layout, wheel design, support components, hardware integration, physical prototyping, and real-world testing. This project combined CAD development with hands-on assembly and performance evaluation.

SolidWorks Mechanical Design Prototyping Testing Assembly Motor Control

Project Overview

This project centered on a compact balancing car platform with custom wheel geometry, support parts, and a lightweight frame arrangement. The work involved CAD modeling, component iteration, physical assembly, and testing of the drive system and overall vehicle behavior.

What I Worked On

  • Modeled the car frame, wheels, spacer, and axle mount components
  • Created top, front, and assembled CAD views to refine layout and fit
  • Built and tested the physical prototype to evaluate motion and control
  • Compared the CAD concept to real fabrication and final assembly

GD&T and SolidWorks

Every component in the full turbine assembly was designed for manufacturability and performance or specified for intended purpose. Technical drawings help during machining with proper dimensioning and tolerances.

From CAD to Physical System

This stage of the project focused on translating the digital design into a functional physical system. Each component had to be designed with both motion constraints and manufacturability in mind.

The interaction between parts was critical, especially in maintaining smooth linkage motion and ensuring structural stability. Iterating between CAD and physical assembly helped refine tolerances and improve overall performance.

What I Learned & Future Improvements

This project strengthened my ability to design for manufacturability while balancing precision and practicality. I gained hands-on experience with CNC machining workflows, including proper setup, toolpath planning, and tolerancing for real components. Working through the full process—from CAD to machining to assembly—highlighted how small design decisions directly impact fabrication efficiency and final fit.
