Motorized Trash Can

A motorized trash can concept focused on linkage motion, assembly layout, front and side configuration, and illustrated motion refinement. The project combined CAD development with mechanism studies to better understand how the opening system could be packaged and actuated.

SolidWorks Mechanism Design Linkage Analysis CAD Assembly Motion Study Concept Development

Project Overview

This project explored a motorized trash can system through CAD assembly, mechanism layout, and linkage-based motion concepts. The work focused on how the moving components interact, how the assembly is packaged, and how the mechanism could be communicated through both geometry and illustrations.

What I Worked On

  • Built assembly views showing the full trash can mechanism concept
  • Developed front and side linkage studies to understand motion paths
  • Created refined visual illustrations to better communicate the design
  • Used mechanism drawings and motion clips to compare concepts and geometry

GD&T and SolidWorks

Every component in the full turbine assembly was designed for manufacturability and performance or specified for intended purpose. Technical drawings help during machining with proper dimensioning and tolerances.

What I Learned & Future Improvements

This project strengthened my ability to design for manufacturability while balancing precision and practicality. I gained hands-on experience with CNC machining workflows, including proper setup, toolpath planning, and tolerancing for real components. Working through the full process—from CAD to machining to assembly—highlighted how small design decisions directly impact fabrication efficiency and final fit.
