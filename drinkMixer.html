Drink Mixer

A compact drink-mixing prototype featuring a custom frame, slider, spinner, claw, and cup-holding components. The project involved CAD, subsystem design, prototyping, and final assembly of a functional mixer.

SolidWorks Mechanical Design 3D Printing Prototype Build Assembly Electronics

Project Overview

This project focused on designing and assembling a drink mixer with a rigid frame, motion components, cup support geometry, and custom mounts. The work included subsystem layout, individual part modeling, and prototype integration.

What I Worked On

  • Designed the frame, slider, spinner, and motor mount components
  • Created custom parts for mounting, support, and cup interaction
  • Developed the prototype layout and final assembled system
  • Integrated wiring and mechanical subsystems into one build

Product And Prototype

This section highlights the walking table mechanism in motion from multiple views. By placing video on both sides, the central text explains how rotational input is transferred through the linkage system to generate a stable walking gait.

The design was inspired by Strandbeest-style leg geometry, where each leg assembly follows a repeatable path that helps maintain smooth forward motion. Watching the system from both sides makes it easier to understand coordination and support.

What I Learned & Future Improvements

This project strengthened my ability to design for manufacturability while balancing precision and practicality. I gained hands-on experience with CNC machining workflows, including proper setup, toolpath planning, and tolerancing for real components. Working through the full process—from CAD to machining to assembly—highlighted how small design decisions directly impact fabrication efficiency and final fit.
