About Me

Elliot Dy

Mechanical Engineer | Researcher | Builder

I am a mechanical engineering student focused on design, prototyping, controls, and product development. I enjoy turning ideas into physical systems through CAD, fabrication, testing, and iteration.

My work ranges from turbine and energy system concepts to electromechanical devices, motion systems, and research-driven engineering projects. I am especially interested in building things that combine strong technical analysis with clean, practical design.

What I Do

I work on projects involving system design, analysis, manufacturing, and integration. I like solving problems that move from concept sketches to detailed models, prototypes, and final testing.

Interests

My interests include mechanical systems, energy systems, autonomous systems, product development, and applied engineering research. I enjoy work that blends theory with hands-on execution.

Approach

I value clear design thinking, strong attention to detail, and practical iteration. My goal is to make systems that are not only functional, but also well-integrated, manufacturable, and thoughtfully engineered.

Outside of Engineering

Outside of technical work, I enjoy building creative side projects, learning new tools, and exploring ways to improve both design quality and user experience.

My Background

I have developed my experience through academic projects, hands-on design work, and research. Across these experiences, I have built skills in SolidWorks, fabrication planning, embedded systems, experimental work, and engineering communication.

I am motivated by projects that require both careful technical thinking and real-world execution. Whether I am modeling a system, assembling a prototype, or refining a final design, I enjoy the process of making ideas work in practice.
